Privacy, clearly explained.

Last updated: 20 March 2026  ·  Effective date: 20 March 2026

Summary

ClearOut is designed to perform its core scanning and cleaning functions on your device. We do not sell your personal data, we do not use ClearOut for advertising, and we do not operate our own backend to receive your scan results or media files. Some purchase, licensing, update, and review-related functions are provided by Google Play and related Google services under their own terms and privacy policies.

01 — Scope

Who this policy applies to

This privacy policy applies to the Android application ClearOut. It explains what information the app may access on your device, why that access is needed, what is stored locally, and how purchases are handled.

Unless otherwise stated, references to “ClearOut”, “we”, “our”, or “us” mean the publisher of the app, ittoolkit. If your Play Console publisher name differs, replace that name with your exact developer or business identity so your legal pages and store listing match.

02 — Information used by the app

What ClearOut may access on your device

ClearOut needs limited on-device access to provide features such as duplicate photo detection, large-file discovery, unused app detection, notification-volume summaries, storage insights, and optional purchase unlocks.

Category Why it is used
Photos and images Used to identify duplicate, similar, blurry, or screenshot images and present them for your review on-device.
Videos Used to identify large video files and other media items that may be taking up storage space.
Audio files Used to identify large audio files stored on the device where supported by Android permissions.
App usage information Used to estimate which apps appear unused based on Android usage access data, where you choose to grant that access.
Notification source information Used, where enabled by you, to count notification activity by app so ClearOut can highlight high-volume senders. ClearOut is designed to use this feature for app-level counting rather than storing notification message content.
Installed app information visible to Android Used to label apps within the interface and support app-related features such as unused app review.
Storage usage statistics Used to show storage trends, estimate fill rate, and support optional weekly storage reports.
Purchase status Used to determine whether Pro features should be unlocked following a purchase or active subscription made through Google Play.

03 — What ClearOut does not do

What we do not collect or use for our own services

ClearOut is not an advertising platform, social network, or cloud storage service. Core app use does not require you to create a ClearOut account.

Important

We do not use ClearOut to sell personal data, build advertising profiles, or send your scan results, media files, or notification counts to our own external servers. Google Play and related Google services may process purchase, licensing, update, and review interactions under their own policies.

No account signup required for core features
No sale of personal data
No advertising profile building
No third-party ad use of your files
No external upload of scan results to our own backend
No use of your photos or files for model training
No requirement for contact or location access
No use of ClearOut for advertising identifiers

04 — Permissions

Permissions and special access used by ClearOut

Android requires explicit permissions or special settings access for some features. ClearOut requests only the permissions needed to provide the specific features described below.

  • 🖼️
    READ_MEDIA_IMAGES / READ_MEDIA_VIDEO / READ_MEDIA_AUDIO
    Used on supported Android versions to access media needed for duplicate photo detection, large-file scanning, screenshot review, and similar media-cleaning functions.
  • 📁
    READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE
    Used on older Android versions where this permission is still required for media access.
  • 🔔
    POST_NOTIFICATIONS
    Used to send optional notifications such as weekly storage reports or reminders, if you choose to enable them.
  • 📊
    PACKAGE_USAGE_STATS
    Used, where you manually grant usage access in Android settings, to estimate which apps appear unused based on app activity information provided by the Android system.
  • 💬
    BIND_NOTIFICATION_LISTENER_SERVICE
    Used, where you manually enable notification access in Android settings, to support notification-volume summaries by app. ClearOut is designed to use this access for app-level counting rather than persisting the contents of your notifications.
  • 💳
    com.android.vending.BILLING
    Used to support purchases, subscriptions, entitlement checks, and restoration of Pro access through Google Play.

No permission is requested for tracking, advertising, or any purpose beyond the feature reasonably associated with that permission.

05 — Local storage

What ClearOut stores locally on your device

ClearOut stores a limited amount of information locally so features work properly and your preferences can be remembered between sessions.

Stored item Purpose
Purchase and Pro status Used to remember whether Pro features should be available after a Google Play purchase or subscription check.
Recent scan timestamps Used to display when you last scanned and improve the app experience.
Recent storage snapshots Used for storage trend calculations, fill-rate estimates, and optional weekly storage summaries.
Notification preferences Used to remember whether optional notifications are enabled or disabled.
Ignore lists or cleanup exclusions Used to avoid showing items again when you have chosen to ignore them.
Onboarding and settings flags Used to remember whether introductory screens, prompts, or certain app options have already been shown.

This information is stored locally within the app environment on your device. In most cases, it is removed when you uninstall the app, although some records controlled by Android or Google Play may be governed by those services instead.

06 — Purchases and Google Play

How subscriptions and purchases work

ClearOut may offer paid features through Google Play, including subscriptions and, where configured, one-time purchases. All payment processing, billing, refunds where available, subscription management, and related account handling are provided by Google Play and related Google services.

We do not receive or store your full payment card details through ClearOut. Product availability, pricing, billing periods, and any free trial configuration may vary by market and by the products configured in Google Play Console.

If you purchase ClearOut Pro, the app may store a local entitlement flag or similar status information so Pro access can remain available without requiring repeated purchase checks every time you open the app.

07 — Sharing and disclosures

When information may be shared

We do not operate a ClearOut backend that receives your scan results, media files, or app-cleaning data for our own services.

However, certain functions necessarily involve platform providers. For example, purchases, licensing, update delivery, store listing interactions, and in-app review flows may involve Google Play or related Google services, which operate under their own legal terms and privacy documentation.

We may also disclose information where required by applicable law, regulation, court order, or lawful governmental request.
