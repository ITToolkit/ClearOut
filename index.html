Summary

ClearOut does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any of your personal data. All scanning happens entirely on your device. We operate no servers. We receive nothing. This policy exists solely to document that commitment.

01 — Data access

What ClearOut accesses on your device

ClearOut reads the following categories of information solely to perform its advertised functions. None of it ever leaves your device.

Data type Purpose
Photos & images Identifies duplicate, blurry, and near-identical images in your photo library, and locates screenshots for review. Files are read locally and never copied or uploaded.
Videos Scans your video library to surface large video files for optional deletion. Video content is never copied, uploaded, or analysed beyond its file name and size.
Audio files Scans for large audio files (e.g. downloaded music, recordings) as part of the large-files scanner. Audio content is never played, copied, or transmitted.
App usage statistics Determines which apps you have not opened recently so ClearOut can surface them as candidates for removal. Usage timestamps remain on your device only.
Notification metadata Counts how many notifications each app delivers per session to identify high-frequency senders. Notification content — the actual text of alerts — is never read, stored, or logged.
External storage & app media folders With All Files Access permission (Android 11+), ClearOut can scan external storage for archives, APK installers, orphaned app folders, and media downloaded by apps such as WhatsApp, Telegram, TikTok, Instagram, and Snapchat. Files are only listed for your review and are deleted only on your instruction.
Installed app list Cross-referenced with your file system to detect residual data folders belonging to apps that have since been uninstalled.
Storage usage & history On each app launch, ClearOut records a snapshot of your total storage used (a single number in bytes) alongside a timestamp. Up to 30 daily snapshots are stored locally to calculate a storage-fill prediction and power weekly notification reports. No file names, paths, or content are included in these snapshots.

02 — What we don't collect

03 — Permissions

Android permissions and why they are required

Android requires explicit permission grants for several of ClearOut's features. Below is a complete list of every permission the app requests and the specific reason each is needed.

  • 🖼️
    READ_MEDIA_IMAGES / READ_MEDIA_VIDEO / READ_MEDIA_AUDIO / READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE
    Grants read access to your photo, video, and audio libraries. Used to identify duplicate images, surface large media files, and scan your screenshots folder. On Android 12 and below, READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE covers all three media types. No media is copied, uploaded, or shared.
  • 📁
    MANAGE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (All Files Access — Android 11+)
    Grants broader access to external storage, enabling ClearOut to scan for archives, APK installers, orphaned app data folders, and media saved by third-party apps (such as WhatsApp, Telegram, TikTok). This permission is granted manually by the user in Android Settings. Files are listed for your review and deleted only on your explicit instruction. Nothing is transmitted externally.
  • 🔔
    POST_NOTIFICATIONS (Android 13+)
    Required to deliver optional weekly storage report notifications to your device. Notifications are generated entirely on-device using locally stored storage snapshots. You can disable these notifications at any time in ClearOut's Settings screen or in your device's system notification settings.
  • 📊
    PACKAGE_USAGE_STATS
    Allows ClearOut to read app launch timestamps — specifically when each app was last opened — to power the unused apps detector. This permission must be granted manually in Android Settings. Usage data is never transmitted.
  • 💬
    BIND_NOTIFICATION_LISTENER_SERVICE
    Enables ClearOut to count notifications per app during the current session. Only the source app's package name is recorded as a local counter — notification text, titles, and content are never read, stored, or transmitted. This permission is granted manually in Android Settings and can be revoked at any time.
  • 📦
    QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES
    Required to retrieve the full list of installed applications. Used to identify orphaned data folders on external storage belonging to apps that have since been uninstalled, and to power the unused apps detector.
  • 💳
    com.android.vending.BILLING
    Required by Google Play to process the ClearOut Pro subscription and lifetime purchase. All payment handling is performed entirely by Google Play's infrastructure. ClearOut never receives, processes, or stores any payment details, card information, or billing addresses.

No permission is used for tracking, advertising, or any purpose beyond the feature explicitly described above.

04 — Subscriptions

ClearOut Pro and in-app purchases

ClearOut Pro is offered as an optional annual subscription (which includes a 7-day free trial) or a one-time lifetime purchase, both available through Google Play. All billing, payment processing, and purchase management is handled entirely by Google Play's infrastructure.

ClearOut does not receive, store, or process any payment information, card details, or billing addresses. For annual subscribers, your subscription status is verified directly between the ClearOut app and Google Play's licensing servers. The free trial converts to a paid subscription automatically at the end of the trial period unless cancelled beforehand. To cancel at any time, visit Google Play → Subscriptions. Lifetime purchases are a one-time charge with no recurring billing.

08 — Local data storage

What ClearOut stores on your device

ClearOut stores a small amount of data in Android's SharedPreferences (an on-device key-value store). This data never leaves your device. It includes:

Stored item Purpose
Pro subscription status A boolean flag indicating whether you have an active Pro subscription or lifetime purchase. Used to unlock Pro features without contacting Google Play on every launch.
Last scan time Timestamp of your most recent scan, displayed on the home screen as "last scanned X minutes ago".
Storage snapshots Up to 30 daily snapshots of your total storage used (a single number in bytes + timestamp). Used exclusively for the storage prediction feature and weekly notification reports. No file names or content are stored.
Notification preference Whether weekly storage report notifications are enabled or disabled.
Cleanup ignore list A list of file URIs you have chosen to exclude from future Quick Clean and Deep Clean suggestions. Stored locally and never transmitted.
Review prompt counter A count of successful cleanup actions and the date of the last in-app review prompt. Used to determine when to show the Google Play review dialog (no more than once every 30 days).
Onboarding status A flag recording whether you have completed the first-launch onboarding flow.

All of the above is stored exclusively on your device in Android's private app storage. It cannot be read by other apps and is deleted when you uninstall ClearOut.
